PC Part Picking

Introduction

Since there are so many different types of pc's (gaming, office, home, professional, etc.) I will focus the following list on building a gaming pc. I have organized the list into what I feel are the most to least important components of building a gaming pc. That being said, every part is important.

Graphics Card

The most important part of a gaming pc is the graphics card. If focused on gaming, spend the most money on this component. At this point in time, Nvidia has released their new RTX 3000 series GPU's and AMD has released their new 6000's series GPU's. If you are looking, for a card in the 400-700 dollar range, I would not recommend any other graphics cards. Cards in the more budget friendly area of $150 to $400 have been on the market for a few years and I expect them to be phased out soon. At his point my recommendation is to wait until new cards are released. If you cannot wait, google "GPU Heirarchy" and find a graphics card that fits your price to performance.

The CPU

The most important part of a cpu when building a gaming pc is making sure that it does not "bottleneck" or make less effective your graphics card. With that being said, you will see performance gains by having a better cpu and a better cpu will be better for all other tasks that your computer will perform. The amount of cores and threads a cpu has, the clock speed, and the amount of processing for those clock speeds is important. For the general gamer, I would recommend a cpu with at least 6 cores. The ryzen 3600 and the ryzen 5600x are good options from AMD on the base level. The ryzen 3700x or ryzen 5700x and up are better high end options. A search for "CPU Hierarchy" and comparing prices to what you are able to pay again is a good option for looking at cpu's.

The SSD

I put the SSD high on this list because it is important for having a responsive pc in this day and age and games are now starting to make use of them to a great extent. An M.2 NVMe SSD with DRAM is going to produce the best results as your main hard drive. A good budget option is the crucial P1 ssd. A good higher end SSD is the Samsung 970 EVO. Looking at the read and write speed of the SSD can be a good indicator of how it will perform. Again, googling "SSD Heirarchy" can net good results.

RAM

When choosing RAM, it is important to look at the size, speed, and timings. For PC's with a Ryzen CPU, the higher MHz the RAM the better. As a general recommendation, I would recommend at leasts 16gb (2 sticks X 8gb) of 3200Mhz with timings of 16-18-18-36. RAM in this price range should cost about $65 which is about $20 more than an 8 gb kit. Higher amounts of RAM like 32gb are important for video editing.

The Motherboard

The Motherboard will need to be compatible with the CPU that you have chosen. Generally, if you go a website like pcpartpicker.com, and use their system builder to select a CPU, it will not let you choose a motherboard that is incompatible. Generally, you should choose a motherboard that supports the RAM speed that you choose as well as gives you the ability to overclock if wanted. I tend to lean toward highly rated budget motherboards so that I can put that money saved towards a better graphics card or CPU.

Power Supply

The Power supply is important for making the components of your pc get enough juice. You can use websites like pcpartpicker to put together your pc build and get a rough idea of how much power your pc will need. It is a good idea to go over that amount. The graphics card that you buy should also give you a recommended power level. One other recommendation is to get a power supply with at least 80+ bronze rating. This rating basically lets you know how well your power supply keeps to it's power standards.

PC Case

When looking for a PC Case, it is important to make sure it has good airflow, as well as areas that will allow you to manage cables. Usually, it is good to look at newer cases that have USB 3.0 ports and a place for the power supply at the bottom. Aside, from that, it is important to get a PC case that looks good. It is the part of the pc that you will see the most and it should be a design that you will not regret.

Monitor

The monitor really should not go at the bottom as it is also an incredibly important part of your computer experience. However, since it is not really part of the tower I put it here. Monitors are seperated into 1080p, 1440p and 4k graphical fidelities. When building your pc, you should really think about what resolution you would like to game at before starting. Make sure to choose a graphics card that will be able to utilize your monitor properly. My biggest recommendation when choosing a monitor is to choose one that has a 144hz or above refresh rate. This allows you to game with a resolution that will better match your framerate and allow for much smoother gameplay. Screen panels like IPS, VA, TN are important terms when looking into monitors. Usually quality goes IPS > VA > TN and so does price. However TN panels have merit in competative gaming scenarios.
